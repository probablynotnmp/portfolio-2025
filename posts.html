So, what exactly is a Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador? How can you become one? What are the benefits? And is it worth your time and effort? Keep reading to find out the answers to these questions!

Before talking about the Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador programme (MLSA) in depth, let's start with the story of how I discovered it.

Discovery

The story began in the year 2022 when I was in my second year of B.Tech. I used to be fairly active on LinkedIn and Microsoft Learn, especially following tech communities and student developers. Around that time, I kept seeing this programme called Microsoft Learn Student Ambassadors pop up on my feed.

It seemed like a very prestigious and competitive student programme. I saw Ambassadors hosting events, conducting workshops, and collaborating with Microsoft. Back then, I assumed this was way out of my league and that only “top” students or those from Tier 1 colleges could get in.

Still, it stayed in the back of my mind.

Fast forward to my third year. One normal day, one of my classmates forwarded a link to the MLSA programme in our class group chat. Out of curiosity, I clicked on it, which turned out to be a turning point.

The link took me to the official Microsoft Learn Student Ambassadors page. I started reading about the programme, its benefits, and how it works.

This time, I didn’t just scroll past it. I thought:

“Why not give it a shot? Even if I don’t get in, at least I’ll know how the process works.”

So I decided I would apply.

My Experience

Before applying, I watched a lot of videos and read blogs about MLSA. Many Ambassadors shared their journeys, their mistakes, and what helped them stand out. This helped me get a clearer picture of what Microsoft was looking for in an applicant.

I learned that it wasn’t just about technical skills or having a high GPA. Instead, it was about:

  • Your passion for technology

  • Your willingness to learn, share, and grow

  • Your involvement in community activities

  • How well you can communicate and present ideas

Looking back, I realised I had already been doing similar things in small ways like helping friends understand topics, sharing resources, and being active in tech communities. That gave me the confidence to move forward with the application.

After I submitted my application, there was a long waiting period. I kept checking my email (more often than I’d like to admit). After a few weeks, I finally received the email I had been hoping for:

I was selected as a Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador.

That moment was surreal. I still remember reading that email again and again to make sure it was real.

From there, the journey only got better.

Benefits

Once I became an MLSA, a lot of doors opened up. Here are some of the most valuable benefits I experienced:

  • Access to learning resources – You get access to a wide range of Microsoft resources, learning content, cloud credits, and tools that help you explore and build real projects.

  • Event opportunities – As an Ambassador, you can host events, workshops, and bootcamps in your college or community. This was one of my favourite parts, as it helped me improve my public speaking and leadership skills.

  • Networking – You get to connect with a global community of student developers, Microsoft professionals, and mentors. This network can be extremely valuable for internships, collaborations, and future career opportunities.

  • Recognition and credibility – Being an MLSA is a strong addition to your profile. It shows that you’re proactive, community-driven, and serious about your growth.

  • Growth to higher milestones – The programme has different milestone levels like Alpha, Beta, and Gold. As you contribute more, you move up these levels and unlock more benefits and recognition.

In my case, after consistent contributions and community work, I was honoured to be recognised as a Gold Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador, one of the first from Kerala.

How to Apply

If you’re thinking of applying to the Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador programme, here’s a simple breakdown of the process:

  1. Go to the official website
    Visit the official MLSA page: https://studentambassadors.microsoft.com/

  2. Check the eligibility
    Make sure you’re currently enrolled in an educational institution (like a college or university) and meet the basic eligibility criteria mentioned on the site.

  3. Sign in with your Microsoft account
    You’ll need a Microsoft account to sign in and start your application. If you don’t have one, you can create it for free.

  4. Fill out the application form
    You’ll be asked about:

    • Your background

    • Your interest in technology

    • Your community involvement

    • Why you want to become an Ambassador

    Be honest and specific. Use real examples from your life.

  5. Record and upload a video
    One of the most important parts of the application is the video. You’ll usually be asked to record a short video (around 2 minutes) answering specific questions.

    Some tips for the video:

    • Speak clearly and confidently

    • Explain who you are, what you do, and why you want to be an MLSA

    • Share how you’ve helped others learn or grow

    • Talk about how you plan to contribute to the community if you’re selected

  6. Submit and wait for the result
    Once you’ve completed all the steps, submit your application and wait for an email from the programme team. This may take a few weeks, so be patient.

Conclusion

Becoming a Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador has been one of the most rewarding parts of my student journey. It pushed me out of my comfort zone, helped me connect with amazing people, and gave me a platform to learn, share, and grow.

If you’re someone who loves technology, enjoys helping others, and wants to be part of a global community, I strongly encourage you to apply.

You don’t need to be the “best” student or know everything. You just need genuine interest, consistency, and the willingness to learn and give back.

Who knows? A single link in a class group chat or a random post on LinkedIn might be the start of your journey too just like it was for me.
